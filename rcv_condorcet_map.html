Ranked-Choice Voting in the United States, 2021-2025

How Good is Instant Runoff Voting?

Instant Runoff Voting (IRV) is the most widely used ranked-choice voting system in the United States. It is designed to reduce vote splitting and elect broadly popular candidates. Yet it can still fail to elect the Condorcet winner, the candidate a majority of voters prefer in head-to-head matchups against every other candidate.

Reading the Map

Each dot is a jurisdiction. Its color shows how IRV's winner compares to the Condorcet winner — the candidate who would beat every other candidate head-to-head. Click any dot to explore its elections.

Green — Agreement

IRV elected the Condorcet winner, and plurality would have too. No difference between methods.

Blue — Condorcet Improvement

IRV elected the Condorcet winner, but plurality would not have. IRV corrected a vote-splitting outcome.

Yellow — Condorcet Failure

A Condorcet winner existed, but IRV eliminated them before the final round. The most broadly preferred candidate lost.

Purple — No Condorcet Winner

Preferences cycle: A beats B, B beats C, C beats A. No candidate is majority-preferred over all others, so any winner is contestable.
